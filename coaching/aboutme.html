My practice is rooted in positive psychology, a branch of psychology that studies what causes people to thrive in their lives and work, and how we can apply those findings in practical ways.

My approach as a coach is to focus on building on the things that are in our control and empower us, such as our strengths and understanding at our core what motivates us.

If you're considering changes in your life or work, or pursuing a significant goal, I believe you'll find my knowledge of positive psychology and my evidence-based approach can help you get the results you want.

I came to this work after extensive study of positive psychology theory and practice, and following years spent working as a teacher and in political organizing.

I hold a BA from Georgetown University and a MS in Education from Fordham University. I've studied positive psychology and the science of effective coaching under Dr. Tal Ben-Shahar, earning a Certificate in Positive Psychology from the WholeBeing Institute. At the WholeBeing Institute I also received instruction as part of the Positive Psychology Coach Training course.
