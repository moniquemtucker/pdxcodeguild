What is Positive Psychology?

by James Heller

I first came across positive psychology a few years ago, and I was puzzled by the term. What is that? Positive thinking? I wanted to learn more so I dug into some articles and books.

Positive psychology essentially focuses on what works. Most research in psychology is on disabling conditions like schizophrenia, depression, and anxiety, whereas positive psychology studies how to build the best things in life, such as love, happiness, joy, and job satisfaction.

Positive psychology also focuses on what works when it comes to its real world applications. For instance, in my coaching practice I generally begin with questions about what is going well, what is working, with the goal of building on those things with clients.

Positive psychology researchers ask: What can we learn from people who thrive? What are the habits and behaviors of people who flourish? From this research come valuable insights and practices that we can use in our lives every day.
